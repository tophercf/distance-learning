With the rise of online courses, many educational institutions have expanded or started a distance learning option.

It's not just Harvard and MIT Either...

Over the span of 2012-2016, the number of institutions that added remote learning opportunities increased by X%. This is an unprecedented shift into alternative learning and based on the trend, it's here to stay.

This trend towards increased remote learning and the ability to take course work from high quality institutes that were previously out of reach due to academic grades or money can also be inferred based on supply side consequencies of this trend. Smaller universities with less prestige are closing at a faster rate, unable to take in the revenues that were previously available due to georgraphic constraints of some students. With a superior complimentary good, we are already seeing consolidation in the market. Moodies predicts this consolidation will continue to force the small universities to close or merge into larger units in order to survive, leading to closures triple of years past.
